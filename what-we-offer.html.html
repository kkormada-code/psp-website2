Innovative capital market instruments structured for your needs

Commercial Paper Issuance

Commercial Paper (CP) is an unsecured, short-term debt instrument, usually issued by corporate entities to raise funds to meet their immediate financial needs...

  • Issuer: Corporate entities with investment grade ratings
  • Investors: Pension funds, insurance companies, banks
  • Tenure: 7 to 270 days
  • Discounted Sale: Sold at a discount to face value
  • Unsecured: Based on issuer's creditworthiness

Corporate Bonds (CB) Issuance

Corporate bonds are long-term debt securities issued by companies to raise funds, promising to repay the principal with interest...

  • Coupon: Fixed interest payments
  • Maturity: At a specified future date
  • Risk: Higher than government bonds, with better returns
  • Purpose: Expansion, refinancing, and project funding

Asset Backed Securities (ABS) Issuance

ABS are securities backed by a pool of underlying income-generating assets, such as mortgages or loans...

  • SPV: Assets are transferred to a separate legal entity
  • Securitization: Pool of loans is converted to tradable securities
  • Tranches: Different risk-return levels
  • Investor Returns: Funded by repayments from borrowers
